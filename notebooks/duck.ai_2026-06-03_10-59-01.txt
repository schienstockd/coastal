## What Contrastive Embeddings Do (CELLECT)

**Contrastive learning** trains a neural network to map each cell into a high-dimensional space where:
- **Similar cells are close together** in that space
- **Different cells are far apart** in that space

Think of it like assigning each cell a unique "fingerprint" in a learned feature space.

### How CELLECT Uses This for Tracking

```
Frame t:
├─ Cell A at position (100, 200) → Embedding vector [0.2, 0.8, 0.1, ...]
├─ Cell B at position (102, 205) → Embedding vector [0.9, 0.1, 0.3, ...]
└─ Cell C at position (500, 600) → Embedding vector [0.3, 0.2, 0.9, ...]

Frame t+1:
├─ Centroid 1 at (101, 202) → Embedding vector [0.2, 0.8, 0.1, ...]  ← Matches Cell A!
├─ Centroid 2 at (103, 207) → Embedding vector [0.9, 0.1, 0.3, ...]  ← Matches Cell B!
└─ Centroid 3 at (502, 602) → Embedding vector [0.3, 0.2, 0.9, ...]  ← Matches Cell C!
```

**The key insight**: Even though centroids are close together (101 vs 103), their embeddings are far apart (different fingerprints). So CELLECT can correctly assign them to the right cells.
